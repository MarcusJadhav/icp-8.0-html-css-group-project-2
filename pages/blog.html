Crystals are beautiful -even in your honey!

 April 24 2024

Have you ever noticed your honey become cloudy or creamy in texture? Don’t worry, it may still be safe to eat! In fact, crystalized and cloudy honey is a sign that it is pure and raw honey - harvested straight from the beehive. This honey is just strained to remove honeycomb and beeswax - nothing else. So how does it crystalize? It is mainly comprised of fructose and glucose (two kinds of natural sugars) and water. When left in a cool, dark place, some of the sugar separates out. It depends on the sugar to water ratio in the honey, and the size of the crystals formed.
